Adoption

How do I adopt a Mèo Ta? +

Adopting a Mèo Ta is easy! Browse our marketplace to find cats available for adoption in your area. Contact the seller or shelter directly through the contact information provided. Most sellers will arrange a meeting so you can interact with the cat before making a decision. Make sure to ask about vaccination records, health history, and any specific care requirements.

What documents do I need for adoption? +

Typical documents needed include:

  • Valid identification (ID card or passport)
  • Proof of residence
  • Vaccination and health records from the seller
  • Adoption agreement (if from a shelter)

Requirements may vary by seller, so always confirm beforehand.

What is the typical adoption fee? +

Adoption fees for Vietnamese cats typically range from 500,000 to 1,500,000 VND, depending on age, breed characteristics, and whether the cat comes from a breeder or shelter. Shelter adoptions are usually more affordable. The fee often covers initial vaccinations, deworming, and health checks.

Taking Care

What should I feed my Vietnamese cat? +

Vietnamese cats thrive on high-quality cat food (wet or dry) specifically formulated for their age group. Feed kittens 3-4 times daily, adult cats twice daily. Always provide fresh water. Avoid human food, especially fish bones, onions, garlic, chocolate, and dairy products. Some owners supplement with cooked chicken or fish occasionally, but commercial cat food should be the primary diet.

How often should I take my cat to the vet? +

Kittens need veterinary visits every 3-4 weeks until 16 weeks old for vaccinations. Adult cats should have annual check-ups for health monitoring and booster vaccinations. Senior cats (7+ years) benefit from bi-annual visits. Schedule immediate vet visits if you notice unusual behavior, loss of appetite, vomiting, diarrhea, or lethargy.

Do Vietnamese cats need special grooming? +

Most Vietnamese cats have short coats requiring minimal grooming. Brush weekly to remove loose fur and reduce hairballs. Long-haired varieties need brushing 2-3 times weekly. Trim nails monthly, clean ears when dirty, and brush teeth 2-3 times weekly with cat toothpaste. Bathing is rarely necessary unless the cat gets very dirty.

What vaccinations does my cat need? +

Essential vaccinations include:

  • FVRCP (feline viral rhinotracheitis, calicivirus, panleukopenia) - starting at 6-8 weeks
  • Rabies - at 12-16 weeks
  • FeLV (feline leukemia) - recommended for outdoor cats

Consult your veterinarian for a complete vaccination schedule tailored to your cat's lifestyle.

Safety

How can I cat-proof my home? +

Cat-proofing essentials:

  • Secure windows and balconies with screens or nets
  • Remove toxic plants (lilies, aloe, pothos)
  • Store medications and cleaning products safely
  • Cover electrical cords or use cord protectors
  • Remove small objects that could be swallowed
  • Secure heavy furniture that could tip over
  • Keep toilet lids closed
Is it safe to let my cat outside in Vietnamese cities? +

In urban Vietnamese areas with heavy traffic, it's safer to keep cats indoors. If you want your cat to experience outdoors, consider supervised outings with a harness and leash, or create a secure enclosed balcony or catio. Indoor cats live longer, healthier lives and are protected from traffic accidents, diseases, parasites, and theft.

What should I do in case of a pet emergency? +

Keep your veterinarian's emergency contact number readily available. In Hanoi and Ho Chi Minh City, several 24-hour emergency vet clinics operate. Signs of emergency include difficulty breathing, seizures, severe bleeding, inability to urinate, suspected poisoning, or extreme lethargy. Transport your cat carefully in a secure carrier and call ahead to the emergency clinic so they can prepare.

For Sellers

How do I list my cat on the marketplace? +

To list your cat, create an account and submit detailed information including age, breed, health status, temperament, and clear photos. Provide vaccination records and be transparent about any health issues. Set a fair price based on the cat's age, breed, and health. Respond promptly to inquiries and be willing to arrange safe meetings with potential adopters.

What information should I provide to potential adopters? +

Essential information includes:

  • Age, gender, and breed characteristics
  • Vaccination and deworming history
  • Spay/neuter status
  • Temperament and behavior patterns
  • Any special needs or health conditions
  • Diet and feeding schedule
  • Whether cat is litter trained
  • Compatibility with children and other pets
How do I ensure safe transactions? +

Safety tips for sellers: Meet in public places or your home (never at the buyer's location initially). Verify buyer's identity. Screen potential adopters by asking about their experience, living situation, and commitment level. Consider a trial period. Request deposit only after meeting in person. Provide a written agreement including health guarantees and return policies. Never ship cats without meeting the buyer.
